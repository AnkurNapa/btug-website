Community standards

Code of Conduct

BTUG is a volunteer-run community built on respect. This applies to everyone — attendees, speakers, sponsors, volunteers, and organizers — at every in-person event, online session, and community channel we run.

Expected behavior

  • Be welcoming and patient — assume good faith and remember everyone was a beginner once.
  • Be respectful of differing viewpoints, experience levels, tools, and lived experiences.
  • Give and accept constructive feedback gracefully; critique ideas, not people.
  • Use inclusive language and respect how people identify, including names and pronouns.
  • Make space for others to speak; credit people for their work and contributions.
  • Respect privacy — ask before sharing photos, datasets, or someone’s contact details.

Unacceptable behavior

  • Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form, online or in person.
  • Sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
  • Demeaning, insulting, or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
  • Public or private harassment, stalking, or following people between channels.
  • Publishing others’ private information (doxxing) without explicit consent.
  • Sustained disruption of talks, workshops, or online discussions.
  • Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behavior.

Reporting a concern

If you experience or witness behavior that breaks this Code of Conduct, please tell us. You do not need to be certain it was a violation — if something felt wrong, we want to hear about it. Reports are handled confidentially by the organizing team.

Email the conduct team

Write to conduct@btug.community, a private inbox monitored by the core organizers. Include what happened, when and where, who was involved, and any context or witnesses.

Confidential form

Prefer not to email? Use the confidential reporting form. You may submit anonymously, though leaving a contact lets us follow up.

What happens after you report

  1. Acknowledge. We aim to confirm we received your report within 48 hours.
  2. Review. The conduct team reviews the report privately, gathers any needed context, and speaks with those involved as appropriate.
  3. Decide. We agree on a proportionate response — this can range from a private warning to removal from an event or a ban from future events and channels.
  4. Follow up. We tell the reporter what action was taken, while protecting the privacy of everyone involved.

Our commitment: we will not name or retaliate against anyone for reporting in good faith. Organizers who are themselves the subject of a report will recuse from handling it. At an in-person event, you can also approach any organizer wearing a volunteer badge for immediate help.

Adapted from the spirit of the Contributor Covenant for our community context.
